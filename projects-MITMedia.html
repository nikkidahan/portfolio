MIT Media Lab

During Fall 2014, I conducted research at MIT Media Labâ€™s Biomechatronics Group. I worked under the guidance of Dr. Hugh Herr (who gave this awesome TED Talk), to research methods to design more usable prosthetics.

The most common problem that users face with lower limb prosthetics is poorly designed sockets, the location where the residual limb connects to the prosthesis. Seamless transition from living tissue to manmade devices is a crucial problem to solve, as this first point of contact determines the entire usability of the device.

We researched methods to reduce this problem by developing more economical and accurate methods to create custom fitted sockets. The ideal socket applies pressure on soft tissue spots to ensure snug fit and relieves pressure on hard, boney locations to decrease pain.

I analyzed images of residual limbs in patients, mapped bone and tissue location, and developed 3D renderings of bone and tissue. The 3D bone-and-tissue rendering allows for an inverse rendering of the socket, matching hard parts of the socket to soft parts of the limb.
