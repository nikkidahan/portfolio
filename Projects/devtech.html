devtech research group

I've always been an advocate for science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) education, so during the summer of 2012 I decided to teach young students how to build and program robots. We taught them the fundamentals of computer programming using blocks. The students then wrote their own programs using the blocks, and other students had to act out the program, much like a robot would.


They then built their robots out of Legos and motors, and actually programmed them on the computers. The students were obsessed with this new power we seemed to have given them, as they could make their creations move without touching them, but we reminded them that they have the power to do even more using science and technology.
