central america service trip

I went on multiple trips to Central America to do community service. We refurbrished a rural school house, built a tree nursery and installed camera traps in the rain forest.While working at the elementary school, we met a 16 year old boy who suffered from meningitis as a baby. He told us that because his mother couldn't afford the care he needed, he was "too dumb" to be in high school with his peers. We quickly took him under our wing and nicknamed him "Chicken Bros" after his favorite restaurant.When we came back a second time, Chicken Bros had graduated, but he came back to the elementary school just to see us.

We also built a tree nursury to enable the locals to reforest. The sun is too powerful for the young tree saplings to grow, so we had to build a protective shelter. Once they grow a little, they are then transported to live in the forest, and more saplings are introduced into the nursery.


We installed camera traps in the rain forest to monitor the wildlife species in the area. The place we were staying was right between two major national parks, and acts as a bridge between those two parks. We discovered a puma, a jaguar and an ocelot on the camera traps. Seeing these large predators meant that the ecosystem was thriving. However, we also discovered three poachers. We promptly notified the police, and the poachers were immediately found and arrested!
